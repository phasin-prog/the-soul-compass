# The Soul's Compass — Resources & Reading Guide

**Route:** `/[locale]/resources`  
**Purpose:** Curated reading guide for visitors entering depth psychology seriously

---

## How to Use This Guide

This is not a bibliography. It is a reading architecture — grouped by entry point, depth level, and theoretical lineage. Start where your interest begins.

---

## Analytical Psychology (Jung)

### Entry Level
| Title | Author | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| *Man and His Symbols* | C.G. Jung (ed.) | Best general introduction; heavily illustrated |
| *Jung: A Very Short Introduction* | Anthony Stevens | Compact academic overview |

### Core Texts
| Title | Author | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| *Memories, Dreams, Reflections* | C.G. Jung | Autobiographical; essential for context |
| *Two Essays on Analytical Psychology* | C.G. Jung | Ego, persona, shadow, anima/animus |
| *Psychological Types* | C.G. Jung | Original typology — read before any MBTI material |
| *The Archetypes and the Collective Unconscious* | C.G. Jung (CW Vol. 9i) | Core archetype theory |
| *Aion* | C.G. Jung | Self, shadow, Christ as symbol |

### Secondary / Commentary
| Title | Author | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| *The Portable Jung* | Joseph Campbell (ed.) | Good anthology for selective reading |
| *Jung and the Making of Modern Psychology* | Sonu Shamdasani | Rigorous intellectual history |
| *Ego and Archetype* | Edward Edinger | Individuation process in detail |

## Psychoanalysis

### Freud
| Title | Author | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| *Introductory Lectures on Psychoanalysis* | Sigmund Freud | Best starting point |
| *The Interpretation of Dreams* | Sigmund Freud | Foundational; Part I sufficient for most readers |
| *Beyond the Pleasure Principle* | Sigmund Freud | Death drive; short and important |
| *Civilization and Its Discontents* | Sigmund Freud | Psyche and culture |

### Adler
| Title | Author | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| *Understanding Human Nature* | Alfred Adler | Core individual psychology |
| *The Practice and Theory of Individual Psychology* | Alfred Adler | Inferiority, social interest, life style |
| *Alfred Adler: A Biography* | Phyllis Bottome | Accessible entry to his life and ideas |

### Lacan
| Title | Author | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| *How to Read Lacan* | Slavoj Žižek | Best beginner entry |
| *Écrits: A Selection* | Jacques Lacan | Difficult; read after secondary literature |
| *The Lacanian Subject* | Bruce Fink | Clear secondary guide |
| *Lacan: A Beginner's Guide* | Lionel Bailly | Accessible overview |

## Typology (Critical Reading)

| Title | Author | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| *Psychological Types* | C.G. Jung | The actual source — not 16 types |
| *Personality Type: An Owner's Manual* | Lenore Thomson | Serious Jungian function analysis |
| *The MBTI Manual* | Myers, McCaulley et al. | Read to understand what MBTI claims — then compare to Jung |
| *Gifts Differing* | Isabel Briggs Myers | MBTI origin text |

> **Note:** Reading Jung's original typology alongside MBTI sources reveals the substantial distance between them. This platform documents that distance — it does not validate MBTI as Jungian theory.

## Reading Paths by Entry Point

**"I came from MBTI and want to understand Jung properly"**  
→ *Psychological Types* (selective) → *Two Essays* → *Man and His Symbols*

**"I want to understand the unconscious from the beginning"**  
→ Freud *Introductory Lectures* → Jung *Two Essays* → Adler *Understanding Human Nature*

**"I'm interested in Lacan"**  
→ Žižek *How to Read Lacan* → Fink *The Lacanian Subject* → Lacan *Écrits* (selected)

**"I want to understand the philosophical background"**  
→ Camus *Myth of Sisyphus* → Sartre *Being and Nothingness* (Part 1) → Heidegger *Being and Time* (Div. I)
